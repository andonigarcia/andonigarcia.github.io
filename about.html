Who I Am!

I am a junior at the University of Chicago studying biology, mathematics, and computer science. I am currently a front-end developer, but also am learning some back-end and iOS development. Feel free to check out my projects and follow-me on various social media. Below is my entire story. It's kind of long, so feel free to read it at will. I just wanted to add it for full disclosure to anyone wanting to know who I am and what I do.

~~~~~

I matriculated into University of Chicago as a die hard bio major, interested in genetics, stem-cells, and cancer biology. As such, before enrolling in college, I talked with many industry professionals in order to figure out which courses and which routes I should take to achieve a career in my interests. They all highly recommended having some CS experience on my resume, because those fields are all data-intensive. Me, being me, took this one step further and came into college predetermined to minor in CS. I was always good at math and it interested me, so I kept that around as my second-major. Thus, I began college as a prospective double major with a minor, intending to do biological research.

During my first year at college, I began an internship in a cellular oncology lab -- just what I was interested in! After a year in the lab, I already began to notice how slow bench-work was and how tedious actual biological research could be. Concurrently, during my second-year, I embarked on my CS-minor. My intro courses proved to be highly fascinating and my interests quickly shifted gears. I began working harder on my compsci stuff and putting my bio work on the backburner.

Here I am today. Still double-majoring in Math and Bio and only minoring in Computer Science; however, I now spend almost all my free time delving into the wonderful world of tech. I have taken extremely interesting and rigorous elective courses in CS, began a rich endeavor into Web Development, and am in the initial stages of a tech-startup. I am versed in emerging technologies, am an active participant in the tech-space and am a member of a large coworking space in Chicago, specifically designed for tech startups. Further, I am now looking towards and applying for tech internships this following summer, instead of my traditional research fellowships in bio labs. I have turned a full 180 and have truly integrated myself into the tech scene.

This is not to say that I am flaky on my bio research or that I am just another person who switches majors in college. I am still extremely interested in biology. But I have found that a career in research -- a lifestyle living by the bench and embedded into academia -- is too mundane to inspire me. I still love to read the newest papers in Cell and get really into my cool bio courses. But, I just don't get inspired to go to my lab job every day. I don't get passionate about the tedious work of replicating lengthy experiments and the slow process of academic research. At least, not in the same way that I am passionate and driven for tech these days. Nowadays, when I embark on a cool tech project, my mind becomes solely focused on that one task for the next couple of days. Recently, when I began my Markov Babbler in JS project, I spent the next 48 hours constantly working on it, ignoring my homework and other duties because I was so obsessed with the project infront of me. That kind of passion and instant love for my work is what I want to have for the rest of my life. That's why I am now emerging in the tech space instead of continuing with my prior biology work.

That is my story. Uner the 'My Resume' tab, you can see everything I've done in each aspect of my professional life. If you have any questions, comments, or just want to talk to me, feel free to reach out. I'm a very personable guy.
